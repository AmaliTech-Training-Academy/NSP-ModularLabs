# Lab 2: User Research Bias

---

## Learning Objectives
- Identify different types of bias that can influence user research findings.  
- Critically analyze existing user research (your previous interviews specifically) to identify potential biases.  
- Develop strategies to mitigate bias in future user research projects.  
- Foster teamwork and communication skills through group discussions.  

---

## Activity Outline

### 1. Material Selection
Provide a selection of existing user research findings related to design, specifically focusing on interview data from the previous assignment (if applicable) or case studies with interview components.

---

### 2. Bias Identification
Each group will analyze the provided research materials, focusing on identifying potential biases that might have influenced the findings.

You may consider the following types of bias:

- **Interviewer Bias:** Unconscious interviewer behavior influencing participant responses (e.g., leading questions, positive reinforcement).  
- **Participant Bias:** Participants providing socially desirable answers or focusing on recent experiences.  
- **Sampling Bias:** Recruiting participants who are not representative of the target user group.  

---

### 3. Mitigating Bias Strategies
Each group will brainstorm and develop strategies for mitigating bias in future user research projects, particularly focusing on interview techniques.

---

### 4. Group Report & Presentation
Each group will prepare a short report summarizing:
- Their analysis of potential biases in the research materials  
- Their proposed strategies for mitigating bias in future projects  

---

## Additional Tips
- Consider the ethical implications of user research and unbiased data collection.
