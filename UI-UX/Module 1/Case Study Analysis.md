# Lab 1: Case Study Analysis

---

##  Learning Objectives

- Analyze the User Interface (UI) and User Experience (UX) of a digital product  
- Identify strengths and weaknesses in UI and UX design  
- Apply UI/UX design principles to propose improvements  
- Develop design concepts (on paper or digitally) to communicate redesign ideas  
- Deliver a clear and concise presentation explaining design decisions  
- Reflect on the learning process and apply UI/UX principles to future projects  

---

## Activity Outline

### 1. Product Selection
Choose **two (2)** familiar digital products:
- One with **good UX**
- One with **bad UX**

> These can include websites, mobile apps, or software that are easily accessible.

---

### 2. Evaluation Phase

#### 🔹 UI Components
Analyze the following:
- Visual elements  
- Layout  
- Navigation  
- Interactive elements (buttons, forms)  

Evaluate based on:
- Clarity  
- Usability  
- Aesthetics  

#### 🔹 UX Components
Assess:
- Overall user journey  
- Ease of use  
- Accessibility  
- Effectiveness in meeting user needs  

---

### 3. Strengths & Weaknesses
- Identify specific **strengths and weaknesses**  
- Provide **clear explanations** to support your analysis  

---

### 4. Design Creation

Based on your evaluation:
- Propose UI/UX improvements  
- Redesign **one screen or user flow** from your *bad UX* example  

####  Option A: Paper Sketch
- Draw your redesign on paper  
- Focus on layout, hierarchy, and navigation  
- Take a clear photo and submit  

####  Option B: Digital Wireframe
- Use tools like Figma (or any preferred tool)  
- Create a **low-fidelity wireframe**  
  - No need for colors, icons, or detailed visuals  
- Show how your design solves the identified problems  

---

### 5. Presentation

Your presentation should include:
- Explanation of your design decisions (rationale)  
- Highlight of improvements made to UI elements  
- Discussion of potential challenges or limitations  

---

##  Submission Notes
- Ensure clarity in your analysis and redesign  
- Keep your presentation concise and well-structured  
- Focus on applying UI/UX principles effectively
